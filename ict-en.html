IT Accessibility Office

The IT Accessibility Office continues to be a provider of adaptive technology and advocate for inclusiveness of people with disabilities in the workplace. We are demonstrated leaders, committed to our accessibility mandate, continuing to provide support to employees with disabilities. In accordance with the Accessible Canada Act and in collaboration with our partners in central agencies, we are recommending internationally recognized standards, guidelines and best practices.

Our services span topics such as procurement, training, awareness, product assessments as well as expert advice on Accessibile Information and Communication Technology (ICT).

What is accessibility?

Accessibility is the word used to describe whether people of all abilities and disabilities can use a product (for example, a website, mobile site or application). A product will contain content, which refers to the information such as:

  • Text, images, and sounds
  • Code or markup that defines structure, presentation, etc. or
  • Information organization in the form of user interface and user experience

Accessibility Compliance Checklist

This document is a preliminary checklist that must be completed to ascertain if accessibility is required for your project. The goal of this document is to help identify potential accessibility requirements; and acts as a point of first contact for managers and the IT Accessibility Office (ITAO).

Document Guide

To best utilize this document, follow each step in order and consult the Supporting Information section for more information on accessibility. Please contact the the ICT Accessibility Audit and Strategy (ICT AAS) team for information pertaining to this document.

Getting Started

  • Fill out the accessibility questionnaire in the next section. If any questions apply to your project, continue to the next steps.
  • Review supporting information related to accessibility standards and policies
  • Send the accessibility questionnaire documents to ICTAAS team
  • Contact IT Accessibility Office to register for an accessibility coaching session if required

Accessibility questionnaire instructions

  • Please go to the ICT accessibility requirements wizard tool
  • Complete to the best of your knowledge Step 1: Define ICT functionality
  • Go to Step 3 Generate requirements document
  • Click on Full requirements table with informative sections (English) and save document
  • Send and email to ICTAAS team with the attached document
